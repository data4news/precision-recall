Step 05

False Positives — noise in the shortlist

The red dots are False Positives: reviews in the editors' shortlist that turn out to be irrelevant. If the LLM flags 5,000 reviews out of 130,000, and precision is low, editors could spend weeks reading dead ends.

In this sample: 14 reviews the LLM sent to editors had nothing to do with unwanted sexual content.

14
False Positives (FP)

Example: "Boobies" — one word, no real complaint. The LLM flagged it. An editor reads it and moves on, having learned nothing useful.

Step 06

False Negatives — buried evidence

The yellow dots are False Negatives: reviews describing real unwanted sexual encounters that the LLM buried in the discard pile. They never reach an editor's desk.

In the full 130,000-review corpus, if recall is 88%, that means roughly 12% of all relevant reviews are silently discarded — and the editorial team has no way of knowing they exist.

5
False Negatives (FN) in sample

Example: "Server issues constantly and too many perverts" — human-labeled as relevant, but the LLM decided "perverts" wasn't explicit enough. It never reached an editor.

Step 07

Precision: is the editor's time well spent?

Of the 50 reviews the LLM sent to editors, how many actually turned out to be relevant?

36 useful reviews (True Positives)
14 dead ends (False Positives)
Precision = TP ÷ ( TP + FP )
= 36 ÷ ( 36 + 14 ) = 36 ÷ 50
72%
72 out of every 100 flagged reviews were actually relevant

At 72% precision, roughly 1 in 4 reviews editors read is a dead end. Manageable at small scale — but if the LLM surfaces 5,000 reviews from the full corpus, that's over 1,000 wasted reads. Low enough precision and the shortlist becomes too noisy to work from.

Step 08

Recall: how complete is the story?

Now look at all the orange dots — the 41 reviews editors would want to find. How many did the LLM surface?

36 surfaced (True Positives)
5 silently buried (False Negatives)
Recall = TP ÷ ( TP + FN )
= 36 ÷ ( 36 + 5 ) = 36 ÷ 41
88%
The LLM surfaced 88% of the relevant reviews

At 88% recall, roughly 1 in 8 relevant reviews across the full 130,000-review corpus is silently discarded. If recall were 50%, editors would be working from half the available evidence — writing a story about a problem that is twice as large as they realize.

Step 09

The trade-off

Precision and recall pull in opposite directions. Tune the LLM to flag more aggressively — recall climbs, editors see more relevant reviews, but the shortlist fills with noise. Tune it conservative — cleaner shortlist, but evidence goes missing across 130,000 reviews.

Which failure hurts more depends on what you're trying to do:

Low precision → editors' shortlist is flooded with irrelevant reviews; the team burns out wading through noise before the story gets written

Low recall → thousands of relevant reviews are discarded unread; the investigation documents a fraction of the real problem and doesn't know it

For investigative journalism, low recall is often the graver risk. You don't know what you don't know. A story built on incomplete evidence may understate the scale of harm — and no editor will realize the gap exists.
